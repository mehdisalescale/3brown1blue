# Free Books for the MML Study Path

## Verified-Free Books by Phase

### Phase 0 — Mathematical Foundations (Pre-MML)

**Mathematics for Machine Learning** — Deisenroth, Faisal, Ong (Cambridge, 2020)
URL: https://mml-book.github.io/ | PDF: https://mml-book.github.io/book/mml-book.pdf
The primary text for this curriculum. Authors commit to keeping the PDF free indefinitely; a separate "printed edition" PDF is also hosted. Covers all 12 chapters listed below.

**Convex Optimization** — Boyd & Vandenberghe (Cambridge, 2004)
URL: https://web.stanford.edu/~boyd/cvxbook/
Full PDF freely hosted with Cambridge's permission. Essential companion for MML Ch. 7 (Continuous Optimization). Authors also provide lecture slides and additional exercises.

**Numerical Linear Algebra** — Trefethen & Bau (SIAM, 1997)
Status: NOT freely available. SIAM sells this as a paid publication; no official free PDF exists. Dropped from list — recommend borrowing from a library instead.

**Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms** — MacKay (Cambridge, 2003)
URL: https://www.inference.org.uk/itprnn/book.pdf
On-screen viewing explicitly permitted by the author; printing not permitted. Covers Bayesian inference, probability, and learning — deep companion to MML Ch. 6 and 11.

### Phase 1 — Core ML Theory

**Understanding Machine Learning: From Theory to Algorithms** — Shalev-Shwartz & Ben-David (Cambridge, 2014)
URL: https://www.cs.huji.ac.il/~shais/UnderstandingMachineLearning/understanding-machine-learning-theory-algorithms.pdf
PDF hosted by the authors at Hebrew University, marked "personal use only, not for distribution." Freely readable; covers SVM theory (MML Ch. 12) and PAC learning rigorously.

**The Elements of Statistical Learning** — Hastie, Tibshirani & Friedman, 2nd ed. (Springer, corrected 12th printing 2017)
URL: https://hastie.su.domains/ElemStatLearn/download.html
Full PDF freely hosted with Springer's permission. Advanced reference for regression, dimensionality reduction, and classification chapters.

**An Introduction to Statistical Learning, Python Edition (ISLP)** — James et al. (Springer, 2023)
URL: https://www.statlearning.com/
Official free PDF download confirmed. Python edition (2023) is the most current version. Gentler entry point than ESL; covers linear models, classification, resampling, and trees with sklearn code.

**Bayesian Reasoning and Machine Learning** — Barber (Cambridge, 2012)
URL: http://web4.cs.ucl.ac.uk/staff/D.Barber/textbook/020217.pdf
PDF hosted on the author's UCL page, freely downloadable. Comprehensive graphical models treatment; pairs well with MML Ch. 6 and 11.

**Probabilistic Machine Learning: An Introduction (Book 1)** — Murphy (MIT Press draft, updated 2025-04-18)
URL: https://probml.github.io/pml-book/book1.html | PDF on GitHub releases
CC-BY-NC-ND license. Covers probability, statistics, linear models, neural nets. Modern Bayesian ML perspective.

**Probabilistic Machine Learning: Advanced Topics (Book 2)** — Murphy (MIT Press draft)
URL: https://probml.github.io/pml-book/book2.html | PDF on GitHub releases
CC-BY-NC-ND license. Deep probabilistic models, approximate inference, generative models.

### Phase 2 — Deep Learning

**Deep Learning** — Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville (MIT Press, 2016)
URL: https://www.deeplearningbook.org/
HTML-only (MIT Press contract prohibits easily copied formats). Full text free online; no PDF. Authors' note: "will remain available online for free." Chrome print-to-PDF works for personal use.

**Neural Networks and Deep Learning** — Nielsen (2019)
URL: http://neuralnetworksanddeeplearning.com/ (TLS cert issue on fetch; confirmed free via prior knowledge and search — author's site, CC license)
Chapter-by-chapter HTML. Exceptionally clear exposition of backpropagation and CNNs for newcomers.

**Dive into Deep Learning (D2L)** — Zhang et al. (Cambridge, v1.0.3)
URL: https://d2l.ai/
Fully free, interactive Jupyter notebooks. Covers PyTorch, JAX, NumPy/MXNet, and TensorFlow implementations side by side. Bridges theory and practice for MML Chs. 8–12.

**Deep Learning for Coders with fastai and PyTorch** — Howard & Gugger (O'Reilly, 2020)
URL: https://course.fast.ai/Resources/book.html | Notebooks: https://github.com/fastai/fastbook
Full book readable free via GitHub Jupyter notebooks and Google Colab. Practical top-down approach; strong complement after finishing MML foundations.

### Phase 3 — Applied Python & Data Science

**Python Data Science Handbook** — VanderPlas (O'Reilly, 2016)
URL: https://jakevdp.github.io/PythonDataScienceHandbook/
Full content on GitHub under CC-BY-NC-ND. Covers NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, and scikit-learn — the exact libraries used in MML chapter exercises.

**Think Bayes, 2nd ed.** — Downey (Green Tea Press / O'Reilly, 2021)
URL: https://allendowney.github.io/ThinkBayes2/
Free online with Jupyter notebooks under CC license. Computational Bayesian inference in Python; natural sequel to MML Ch. 6 and 11.

**Think Stats, 3rd ed.** — Downey (O'Reilly, 2024)
URL: https://allendowney.github.io/ThinkStats/
Free online with Jupyter notebooks under CC license. Exploratory data analysis and statistics in Python.

### Phase 4 — NLP & Sequence Modeling

**Speech and Language Processing, 3rd ed. draft** — Jurafsky & Martin (Jan 6, 2026 draft)
URL: https://web.stanford.edu/~jurafsky/slp3/
Full free draft PDF (all 25 chapters + appendices). Authors explicitly invite classroom use. Covers transformers, LLMs, sequence models thoroughly.

**Hugging Face LLM Course** (formerly NLP Course) — Hugging Face team (2022, updated 2024–2026)
URL: https://huggingface.co/learn/nlp-course/chapter1/1
Apache 2.0 license. Free, no ads. Covers Transformers library, fine-tuning, datasets, tokenizers, and advanced LLM topics. Practical code-first complement to Jurafsky & Martin.

### Phase 5 — Reinforcement Learning

**Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction, 2nd ed.** — Sutton & Barto (MIT Press, 2018)
URL: http://incompleteideas.net/book/the-book-2nd.html
Complete PDF freely hosted by the authors at incompleteideas.net. Standard RL reference.

## Dropped Books

**Numerical Linear Algebra** — Trefethen & Bau (SIAM, 1997): No free PDF available from SIAM or the authors. Dropped. Recommend a library copy or use MML's linear algebra chapters + NumPy documentation instead.

**Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning** — Bishop (Springer, 2006): A PDF has been hosted on the Microsoft Research page and is widely mirrored, but Microsoft Research's page as of verification does not explicitly declare it officially free — it primarily links to commercial purchase options. Murphy's PML books (above) are the modern open replacement and are explicitly CC-licensed. Dropped from primary list; treat any hosted copy as use-at-own-discretion.

**Build a Large Language Model From Scratch** — Raschka (Manning, 2024): No free chapters or PDF available. The author offers a free YouTube playlist covering the same content. Dropped. Link to playlist if needed: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLTKMiZHVd_2IIEsoJrWACkIxLRdfMlw11

## Added Beyond Candidate List

**Deep Learning for Coders with fastai and PyTorch** (Howard & Gugger, 2020): Added because it is fully free via GitHub notebooks and provides the top-down practical coding perspective missing from the other books. Strong bridge between MML theory and real implementation.

**Hugging Face LLM Course** (2022–2026): Added because it is fully free (Apache 2.0), actively maintained through 2026, and covers the transformer ecosystem with running code — the most practical NLP entry point beyond Jurafsky & Martin.

## MML Chapter → Python Library Mapping

| MML Chapter | Key Libraries / Functions | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Ch. 2 Linear Algebra | `numpy.linalg`, `numpy.dot`, `scipy.linalg` | Matrix ops, solving linear systems, dot products in any ML pipeline |
| Ch. 2 Linear Algebra | `sympy.Matrix` | Symbolic verification of rank, null space, eigenvalues during study |
| Ch. 3 Analytic Geometry | `numpy.linalg.norm`, `numpy.inner` | Computing norms, inner products, angles between vectors |
| Ch. 3 Analytic Geometry | `sklearn.metrics.pairwise` | Pairwise distance/similarity matrices in kernel and kNN methods |
| Ch. 4 Matrix Decompositions | `numpy.linalg.eig`, `numpy.linalg.svd` | Eigendecomposition and SVD for PCA, spectral methods |
| Ch. 4 Matrix Decompositions | `scipy.linalg.cholesky`, `scipy.linalg.lu` | Cholesky for Gaussian models; LU for solving linear systems efficiently |
| Ch. 5 Vector Calculus | `sympy.diff`, `sympy.jacobian` | Symbolic differentiation to verify gradient derivations by hand |
| Ch. 5 Vector Calculus | `jax.grad`, `jax.jacobian` | Automatic differentiation for custom loss functions and neural nets |
| Ch. 6 Probability and Distributions | `scipy.stats` (norm, beta, dirichlet, …) | Sampling, PDF/CDF evaluation, fitting for any probabilistic model |
| Ch. 6 Probability and Distributions | `numpy.random`, `torch.distributions` | Vectorized sampling; `torch.distributions` for reparameterization trick |
| Ch. 7 Continuous Optimization | `scipy.optimize.minimize` | Gradient-free and gradient-based minimization for small-scale problems |
| Ch. 7 Continuous Optimization | `torch.optim` (SGD, Adam, LBFGS) | Gradient descent variants when the objective is differentiable via autograd |
| Ch. 8 When Models Meet Data | `sklearn.model_selection` (cross_val_score, GridSearchCV) | Estimating generalization, hyperparameter search, bias-variance tradeoff |
| Ch. 8 When Models Meet Data | `sklearn.pipeline.Pipeline` | Chaining preprocessing and estimators to avoid data leakage |
| Ch. 9 Linear Regression | `sklearn.linear_model` (LinearRegression, Ridge, Lasso) | Fitting and regularizing linear models; Ridge = L2, Lasso = L1 |
| Ch. 9 Linear Regression | `numpy.linalg.lstsq`, `statsmodels.OLS` | Direct least-squares solve; statsmodels for confidence intervals and diagnostics |
| Ch. 10 PCA | `sklearn.decomposition.PCA`, `sklearn.decomposition.TruncatedSVD` | Dimensionality reduction; TruncatedSVD for sparse data |
| Ch. 10 PCA | `numpy.linalg.svd`, `numpy.linalg.eigh` | Manual PCA to verify the textbook derivation step by step |
| Ch. 11 Density Estimation / GMMs | `sklearn.mixture.GaussianMixture` | Fit GMMs with EM; choose components via BIC/AIC |
| Ch. 11 Density Estimation / GMMs | `scipy.stats.gaussian_kde`, `sklearn.neighbors.KernelDensity` | Non-parametric density estimation when GMM assumptions are too strong |
| Ch. 12 Classification / SVMs | `sklearn.svm.SVC`, `sklearn.svm.LinearSVC` | Kernel and linear SVMs; `SVC` for small datasets, `LinearSVC` for scale |
| Ch. 12 Classification / SVMs | `sklearn.preprocessing.StandardScaler` | SVM is not scale-invariant — always scale features before fitting |
